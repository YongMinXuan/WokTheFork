Crazy Rich Asians Food Tour

Duration: 5 hours
"Not here for a long time, but for a forkking good time."

In the widely acclaimed Hollywood blockbuster, Crazy Rich Asians, food was featured throughout the entire movie. Warner Bros., the studio that made Crazy Rich Asians, just released this behind-the-scenes featurette about the filming of that sequence, with insights from the cast and crew. “The thing that we think about, when we think about Asia, is street food,” says director Jon M. Chu. “You have all walks of life, all cultures, eating with each other.” The night market dinner shows two worlds colliding in an exciting, seemingly harmonious way, but later in the film, another big food scene — the dumpling standoff — uses food to highlight the tension between Rachel and Nick’s family.

In the early scenes, the main characters of the film were brough to Newton Food Centre where they had local delights such as satay, laksa curry, char kway teow and hokkien mee. Newton Food Centre is one of the most well-known hawker centres in Singapore, you will be sure you won't miss out on it when you join us!

In addition to Newton FC, we will take you to several other hawkers such as Lau Pa Sat and Changi Village that have their own specialties. Each hawker has their own famous dishes that you ought to try, and we know them all!

Not only do we bring you to our favourite hawkers, we also take you to our famous King of the fruits - Durians. Treat yourself to a selection of good class durians (yes they have brandings). However, don't be afraid if you're unable to take the unique smell of durian, there are other tropical fruits like the mangosteens, rambutans and mangoes for you to indulge in.
